    • Cambria California as Told By Shoes
    March 28, 2015
    When Yang Qu though of “X,” she immediately thought of errors. She thought of change or the idea that something is wrong or could be transformed. “One night, I was studying when a moth flew into my room and started fluttering around the lampshade and that’s when I thought, that’s my theme,” said Yang Qu. Yang Qu thought of the life process of a moth. It first goes through a cocoon and then comes out transformed, just like the idea of “X” being transformable. This theme will be clearly visible in Qu’s outerwear designs which incorporate capes and coats. In order to depict the qualities of a moth, the fabrics will mainly consist of wool and sheer cloth with flowers hand embodied onto them by Qu. Qu uses light and airy fabric to capture the cocoon aesthetic including its antennas and textures while also focusing on the fragility of the moth’s wings. The color schemes revolve around dark grey, black and white. “I just really wanted it to be graphic and bold, which is how a moth is perceived in my eyes,” said Qu. Qu is particularly inspired by a specific moth, which acts as the main inspiration to this years design. It’s a moth that started out as white and lived in London. Yet to adapt, they changed from white to black to escape from predators — which made them more vulnerable to predators. The designs inspired by the moth reflect much of Qu’s introverted personality. Qu has the tendency to hide herself sometimes from the world around her. Just like the coats, you can reveal and hide yourself whenever you want to.
